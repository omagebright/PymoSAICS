# Provenance and redistribution notes for the generated parameter decks

This file records where the AMBER-family MOSAICS decks come from and what a user
should cite. It covers the OpenMM-XML-derived decks (`db_ol21_ol3`,
`db_ol15_ol3`, and their `_terminal` variants), the AMBER-prmtop-derived
`OL24/OL3` decks, and, by reference, the baseline `db_bsc1` / `db_bs0` decks.

## What was generated, and from what

The `OL21/OL3` and `OL15/OL3` decks were produced by
`tools/openmm_xml_to_mosaics.py` from the OpenMM force-field XML files shipped
with the OpenMM / openmmforcefields distribution, then overlaid with exact
proper-torsion rows from a matched AMBER `tleap` topology (the `--amber-prmtop`
overlay) so that wildcard and exact torsion precedence match `sander`:

| MOSAICS deck | OpenMM XML sources | AMBER overlay |
|---|---|---|
| `db_ol21_ol3`, `db_ol21_ol3_terminal` | `amber19/DNA.OL21.xml` + `amber14/RNA.OL3.xml` | `leaprc.DNA.OL21` + `leaprc.RNA.OL3` matched 1EFS prmtop |
| `db_ol15_ol3`, `db_ol15_ol3_terminal` | `amber14/DNA.OL15.xml` + `amber14/RNA.OL3.xml` | `leaprc.DNA.OL15` + `leaprc.RNA.OL3` matched 1EFS prmtop |

The converter writes only the additive terms MOSAICS evaluates (bonds, bends,
periodic propers and impropers, Lennard-Jones, 1-4 pairs). Any XML section it
does not convert is recorded in each deck's `manifest.tsv`, not silently
dropped.

The baseline `db_bsc1` / `db_bs0` decks were produced earlier from AMBER frcmod
output via `tools/convert_charmm_tors.pl` and `tools/extract_mol_type_torsions.py`
(see README.md for the bug fixes baked in).

The `OL24/OL3` decks were generated by `tools/amber_prmtop_to_mosaics.py` from
resolved AMBER `prmtop` files. The DNA source files are the official
`ff-DNA-OL24` package (`leap-ff-nucleic-OL24.in`,
`ff-nucleic-OL24.frcmod`, and `ff-nucleic-OL24.lib`). The RNA half is OL3 from
AmberTools. Two MOSAICS profiles are intentionally separate:

| MOSAICS deck | AMBER source route | Intended use |
|---|---|---|
| `db_ol24_ol3` | matched `1EFS` AMBER topology plus internalized DNA/RNA controls, with additional coverage prmtops for static completeness | matched heteroduplex and internalized systems |
| `db_ol24_ol3_terminal` | true-terminal pure DNA/RNA tetranucleotide AMBER topologies | pure DNA/RNA chains with explicit 5-prime and 3-prime terminal chemistry |

The split is required because the resolved AMBER topology can assign different
proper torsion rows to the same atom-type tuple depending on the construction
route. The committed profiles are validated against the route they are intended
to reproduce.

## Underlying force-field references (cite these, not this repo)

The parameter values are the published AMBER force fields. Anyone using these
decks in a publication should cite the original force-field papers, not MOSAICS,
for the parameters themselves:

- parmbsc1 (DNA): Ivani et al. 2016, Nat. Methods, DOI `10.1038/nmeth.3658`.
- parmbsc0 (DNA): Perez et al. 2007, Biophys. J., DOI `10.1529/biophysj.106.097782`.
- OL15 (DNA): Zgarbova et al. 2015, JCTC, DOI `10.1021/acs.jctc.5b00716`.
- OL21 (DNA): Zgarbova et al. 2021, JCTC, DOI `10.1021/acs.jctc.1c00697`.
- OL24 (DNA): cite the OL24 primary publication and the official AMBER
  `ff-DNA-OL24` distribution used for generation. Verify the exact DOI against
  the AmberTools or OL24 source package version used in your release.
- OL3 / chiOL3 (RNA): the AMBER RNA.OL3 corrections distributed with AmberTools.
  Cite the AMBER RNA.OL3 / chiOL3 primary reference as listed on the AMBER
  nucleic-acid models page (verify the exact DOI against the AmberTools version
  you used; it is not asserted here to avoid a wrong citation).

Authoritative upstream sources:

- AMBER nucleic-acid force-field models: `https://ambermd.org/AmberModels_nucleic.php`
- Official AMBER OL24 source package page: `https://ffol.bch.cas.cz/ff-DNA-OL24/`
- OpenMM force-field XML files: the `openmm` / `openmmforcefields` distribution.

## Redistribution

- The MOSAICS decks in this directory are a **derived representation** of the
  above published force fields, reformatted into MOSAICS deck syntax. They carry
  no new parameters of our own.
- OpenMM and openmmforcefields are open-source (MIT-style). AmberTools has its
  own license; the AMBER force-field parameters are published in the literature
  and are freely usable for research with citation.
- Before redistributing these decks outside the research collaboration, confirm
  the license terms of the specific OpenMM and AmberTools versions used, and
  retain the citations above. When in doubt, ship the converter and the source
  pointers rather than only the derived deck, so downstream users can regenerate
  from upstream.

## Reproducing a deck from scratch

See `tools/README.md` (`openmm_xml_to_mosaics.py` and
`amber_prmtop_to_mosaics.py` sections) and README.md ("How decks are
generated"). The OpenMM XML converter plus the matched `--amber-prmtop` overlay
regenerate the OL21/OL3 and OL15/OL3 decks. The AMBER prmtop converter
regenerates the OL24/OL3 decks from resolved AMBER topologies built from the
official OL24 source files.
